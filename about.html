Our Mission

  • To encourage people to donate plants for greater greener impact.
  • To save rare species of plants so that we can save natural gems of heritage.
  • To nurture Mother Nature in her originality so that everyone can thrive harmonally.

Our Vission

  • Preserve Biodiversity.
  • Sustainable land use and protect endangered plant species.
  • Integration of traditional knowledge, educate and outreach.
  • Research, Innovate and long term commitments.

Our Team

Dr. Natrajan, Ph.D.

President & CEO

Dr. Natrajan brings wide-ranging experience in nonprofit conservation leadership, research, and practice to his role as President and CEO of CPC where he oversees CPC National Office operations and staff to further our mission to prevent the extinction of rare plant species.

Extensive involvement in and commitment to the protection of biodiversity by establishing sustainable ecosystems through rare and endangered plant and animal conservation and habitat restoration, and working with communities and governments to improve natural resources management systems.

Hrutava, M.A

Vice President, Science & Conservation

Ms. Hrutava is dedicated to improving access to biological data for conservation research and promoting synthetic research among botanical institutions. Her research applies of data science to plant collections records to develop smarter strategies for collections prioritization and to uncover patterns in rare plant storage behavior in seed banks.

She also develops and maintains the web databases for the seed collections initiative.

Pranoti K, M.A.

Director of Communications & Public Programs

Pranoti K manages marketing and communications initiatives, supports advancement and fundraising activities, and serves as project manager for the upcoming Conservation Course. She also supports web-based educational communications on the Seasonal Plant Care.

Pranoti brings to the team over ten years of experience in the museum industry, and a passion for blending mission-driven content with storytelling to engage, promote, and garner support.

Pritam Shah

Conservation Program associate

Pritam Shah is a recent graduate from Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology. As Conservation Program Associate, he assists with database management for the seed collection initiative, in addition to aiding research projects and data analysis.

Pritam also assists in the development of the online Applied Plant Conservation course and supports marketing and communication efforts by developing content and social media platforms. He is passionate about accessible scientific resources, outreach, and education, and hopes to expand his knowledge in plant conservation and research.

Mohini Singh, Ph.D.

Administrative specialist

As the Administrative Specialist, Mohini Singh supports plant conservation on the business side of the organization. She supports day-to-day operations, the PPP Board of Trustees and the national conference.

Mohini brings a unique background of over 15 years of experience as an administrator of a small nonprofit charter school and an organic chemistry background. She is dedicated to meaningful organizations and thrives when giving back to the community.

Pranav K

Visual Media manager

Pranav K has five years of conservation botany expertise as research associate in the Godrej’s Native Plant Seed Bank. For PPP, Pranav draws upon the conservation experience of his “day job” to create storyboards & edit content for the instructional videos that populate PPP’s IMLS funded learning platform PPP Rare Plant Academy.
